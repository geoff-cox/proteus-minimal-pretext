    Exercise 12. Understanding Newton‚Äôs Update Formula.

      Let \(f(x)\) be a function of \(x\) and let \(x_0\) be some \(x\)-value. Match each expression on the left with its geometric meaning, on the right.
      üîó
    • \begin{equation*} (x_0, f(x_0)) \end{equation*}
    • A point on the graph of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
    • \begin{equation*} f'(x_0) \end{equation*}
    • Slope of the tangent line passing through \((x_0, f(x_0))\text{.}\)
    • \begin{equation*} y = f'(x_0)(x-x_0)+f(x_0) \end{equation*}
    • Equation of the tangent line passing through \((x_0, f(x_0))\text{.}\)
    • \begin{equation*} 0 = f'(x_0)(x-x_0)-f(x_0) \end{equation*}
    • First step in finding the \(x\)-intercept of a line (setting \(y=0\)).
    • \begin{equation*} x = x_0 - \dfrac{f(x_0)}{f'(x_0)} \end{equation*}
    • The (solved) \(x\)-intercept of the tangent line.
    • \begin{equation*} x_1 = x_0 - \dfrac{f(x_0)}{f'(x_0)} \end{equation*}
    • First Approximation in Newton‚Äôs Method.
    üîó

    Exercise 13. Detecting a ‚ÄúBad‚Äù Initial Guess.

    Starting with an initial guess, \(x_0\text{,}\) suppose you compute the next value, \(x_1\text{,}\) in Newton‚Äôs Method using the update formula:
    \begin{equation*} x_{1} = x_{0} - \dfrac{f(x_{0})}{f'(x_{0})} \end{equation*}
    Which of the following might be an indication that you have a ‚Äúbad‚Äù initial guess?
    üîó
    • When computing \(f'(x_0)\text{,}\) you get a value very close to zero.
    • Yes, a very small \(f'(x_0)\) (i.e., denominator in the formula) usually leads to the next approximation being very far from the root.
    • The value of \(x_1\) is very far from \(x_0\text{.}\)
    • Yes, this is a bad sign since it indicates that tangent line passes through a point near a ‚Äúpeak‚Äù or ‚Äúvalley‚Äù of \(f(x)\text{.}\)
    • When computing \(f'(x_1)\) in the formula, you get a value near zero.
    • Since \(f'(x_1)\) is not in the formula for \(x=x_0\text{,}\) it shouldn‚Äôt bepart of the calculation.
    • When computing \(f(x_0)\) in the formula, you get a value near zero.
    • No, this is actually a good sign since \(x_0\) is near the root and Newton‚Äôs method will likely converge.
    • The value of \(x_1\) is very close to \(x_0\text{.}\)
    • No, this is a good sign since the only way this could happen is if \(f(x_0)\) is near zero, which means \(x_0\) is near the root and Newton‚Äôs method will likely converge.
    üîó

    Exercise 14. Where Newton‚Äôs Method Fits.

    Select all situations where Newton‚Äôs Method would be a reasonable tool.
    üîó
    • Approximating the solution to \(\cos(x)=x\text{.}\)
    • Correct‚Äîthis equation cannot be solved algebraically but Newton‚Äôs Method can approximate the solution.
    • Finding the exact solution to \(x^2=9\text{.}\)
    • Not needed‚Äîthis equation has an exact solution, so Newton‚Äôs Method is unnecessary.
    • Programming a calculator or computer to approximate roots efficiently.
    • Correct‚ÄîNewton‚Äôs Method is fast and widely used in computational root finding.
    • Estimating the slope of a tangent line at a point.
    • No‚ÄîNewton‚Äôs Method uses tangent slopes, but its goal is to find roots, not slopes.
