    Chapter 2 Related-Rates Matching Questions

    Checkpoint 2.0.1. Converting Words Into Math.

      Match the mathematical expressions on the left to the corresponding statements about changing quantities in a saltwater tank on the right.
      All quantities are functions of time, \(t\) (min), defined as follows:
      \(V(t)\) Volume of water in a tank (m\(^3\)) at time, \(t\)
      \(h(t)\) Height of water in a tank (m) at time, \(t\)
      \(C(t)\) Concentration of salt in a tank (kg/m\(^3\)) at time, \(t\)
      Hint.
      \(\succ\)
      Some mathematical expressions are not given in any of the statements and more than one can go to the same statement.
      \(\succ\)
      Don’t forget to look at the units!
      \(\succ\)
      Don’t assume the information in different statements are connected.
      Be sure to distinguish between values (like \(h(3)\) or \(C(3)\)) and rates of change (like \(\frac{dV}{dt}(3)\)). Units and context help decide what a statement implies.
    • \(V(1) = 0.7\)
    • \(\text{After}\ 1\ \text{minute, the tank contains}\ 0.7\ \text{m}^3\ \text{of water}\text{.}\)
    • \(V(3) = 1.2\)
    • \(\frac{dV}{dt}(3) = -0.7\)
    • \(\text{After}\ 3\ \text{minutes, the tank contains}\ 1.2\ \text{m}^3\ \text{of water,}\)
      \(\text{but starts draining at}\ 0.7\ \text{m}^3/\text{min}\text{.}\)
    • \(C(3) = 0.8\)
    • \(\text{The salt concentration at}\ t = 3\ \text{is}\ 0.1\ \text{kg/m}^3\text{.}\)
    • \(C(0) = 0\)
    • \(\frac{dC}{dt}(1) = 0.02\)
    • \(\text{Initially, the tank has no salt, but it is added} 1\ \text{minute}\)
      \(\text{later, causing the concentration to begin changing}\)
      \(\text{by}\ 0.02\ \text{kg/m}^3\ \text{per minute}\text{.}\)
    • \(\frac{dV}{dt}(3) = 0.1\)
    • \(\text{At}\ t=1,\ \text{the tank is filling by}\ 0.1\ \text{m}^3/\text{min}\text{.}\)
    • \(\frac{dV}{dt}(3) = 0.7\)
    • \(\frac{dV}{dt}(1) = 0.7\)
