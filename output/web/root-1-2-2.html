      Be sure to distinguish between values (like \(h(3)\) or \(C(3)\)) and rates of change (like \(\frac{dV}{dt}(3)\)). Units and context help decide what a statement implies.
    • \(V(1) = 0.7\)
    • After 1 minute, the tank contains 0.7 m\(^3\) of water.
    • \(V(3) = 1.2\)
    • \(\frac{dV}{dt}(3) = -0.7\)
    • After 3 minutes, the tank contains 1.2 m\(^3\) of water, but drains at 0.7 m\(^3\)/min.
    • \(C(3) = 0.8\)
    • The salt concentration at t = 3 is 0.1 kg/m\(^3\)
    • \(C(0) = 0\)
    • \(\frac{dC}{dt}(1) = 0.02\)
    • Initially, the tank has no salt. After 1 minute, salt is added, increasing the concentration by 0.02 kg/m\(^3\) per minute.
